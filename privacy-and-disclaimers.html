Overview

This report is provided for general informational and educational purposes only. By reading this report, the reader acknowledges and agrees to the notices set out below.

01 Purpose & General Use Statement

This document is intended to support public-interest analysis and policy discussion concerning systemic cybersecurity vulnerability conditions within the US telecommunications sector, based on the synthesis of publicly available information.

The report is designed to support regulatory awareness, program design, and sector-level resilience discussions — including those associated with broadband deployment, infrastructure funding, and operational-sustainability initiatives.

02 Report Scope & Limitations

The analyses and observations contained herein:

  • Were derived exclusively from open-source, publicly disclosed vulnerabilities, cybersecurity advisories, incident reports, domain registry records, public company filings, openly published industry reports and other openly accessible reference material.
  • Reflect aggregate and sector-level data insights, and not evaluations of individual telecommunications providers or networks.
  • Do not constitute explicit findings, determinations, compliance judgments, or lack of enforcement conclusions under any statute, rule, or program, including but not limited to FCC regulations or the BEAD Program requirements.

2.1 Not a vulnerability assessment, audit or compliance evaluation

This report does not constitute, and should not be construed as, a vulnerability assessment, penetration test, security audit, compliance evaluation, threat assessment, incident-response engagement or definitive evaluation of cybersecurity risk for the telecommunications sector or for any individual provider. It reflects a high-level analysis of publicly available information and is not a definitive indicator of risk, exploitability, compliance status, or operational practice.

2.2 Non-intrusive source basis

All information referenced in this report was derived exclusively from publicly accessible, non-intrusive sources. No authenticated scans were attempted against any system, no access controls were circumvented, no proprietary or non-public data was accessed, and no system performance, availability or confidentiality was impacted in connection with the preparation of this report. No representation is made as to the completeness, accuracy, or contemporaneity of third-party disclosures, which may evolve over time and vary in depth or reliability.

03 No Enforcement, Compliance or Eligibility Implication

Nothing in this document should be interpreted as:

  • An allegation of non-compliance, negligence or misconduct by any US telecommunications provider;
  • A basis for enforcement, adjudication, or funding eligibility determination;
  • A substitute for formal regulatory review, audit, or provider self-certification.
No assertions about specific providers

Where examples or aggregate counts are given, they are intended solely to convey industry-level patterns. Any inference drawn about a specific provider, named or unnamed, is made by the reader at the reader’s own risk.

References to technologies, operational environments, or risk categories are contextual and illustrative only and do not imply regulatory interpretation or policy position.

07 Responsible Disclosure & Information Sensitivity

Sensitive technical detail that could reasonably enable malicious activity has been intentionally omitted, summarized or generalized throughout this report. The authors adhere to widely-recognized responsible-disclosure principles consistent with research touching on critical-infrastructure sectors, and do not publish information that could foreseeably facilitate unauthorized access to, exploitation of, or operational disruption of any provider’s systems.

The principles applied during preparation are aligned with the spirit of CISA’s Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure framework and with sector-specific expectations for safeguarding critical-infrastructure information.

Specifically:

  • No exploitation paths, attack instructions, or specific configuration detail are published.
  • All findings are aggregated and presented at the industry level. No individual provider is named, characterized, or evaluated.

Appendix A Legal Risk Considerations

This appendix summarizes the categories of legal risk the authors took into account in preparing this report, together with the corresponding controls applied. It is provided for transparency. Nothing in this appendix constitutes legal advice; readers requiring such advice should consult qualified counsel.

A.1 Computer Fraud & Abuse Act

Federal courts have at times interpreted the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act broadly, including in contexts involving analysis of publicly accessible information about computer systems. To remain well within accepted boundaries, the analysis underlying this report was conducted on a strictly non-intrusive basis. No authentication was attempted against any system. No access controls were circumvented. No proprietary, internal or non-public data was accessed or attempted. No system performance, availability or confidentiality was impacted. The work relied exclusively on openly accessible information and on widely-used reference sources, used in a manner consistent with the published terms governing those sources.

A.2 Digital Millennium Copyright Act §1201

Section 1201 of the Digital Millennium Copyright Act restricts circumvention of technological measures controlling access to copyrighted works. This report contains no exploit code, no bypass technique, and no actionable instruction that could be construed as enabling circumvention of any access control. References to widely-documented vulnerabilities, including CVEs in the CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalogue, are limited to identification by CVE number and to descriptions already in the public domain. Such references are presented exclusively at the industry-aggregate level.

A.3 Defamation & Reputational Harm

Sector-level cybersecurity reporting can give rise to reputational claims if specific operators are characterized in ways they consider inaccurate or harmful. To mitigate this risk, the report does not name any individual provider. All findings are aggregated and reported at the industry level. The report has been drafted to describe observable industry-level conditions — using terms such as “present,” “analysed,” “publicly visible,” and “elevated risk band” — rather than to assert fault or negligence with respect to any operator.

A.4 Critical-Infrastructure Sensitivity

Telecommunications is a designated critical-infrastructure sector under the US Department of Homeland Security sector classification. To respect the sensitivity associated with that designation, sensitive technical detail has been intentionally generalised throughout this report. No IP address, asset-specific identifier, or operator-specific configuration appears in the report, and no exploitation pathway is described. The focus of the report is on aggregate sector posture relevant to policy, board-level and resilience discussion.

A.5 Tool & Source Terms of Use

The analysis underlying this report relied on openly accessible information accessed in a manner consistent with the published terms governing the relevant sources. Where automated retrieval was used, it was operated within the parameters those sources permit. Where reference tools or libraries were used, they were used in accordance with their respective licensing terms.

A.6 Regulatory & Methodological Framework Alignment

The approach taken in preparing this report is consistent in spirit with established frameworks for non-intrusive cybersecurity research, including the passive-assessment principles articulated in NIST Special Publication 800-115, the external attack-surface management principles reflected in CISA Binding Operational Directive 23-02, the best-practice guidance issued by the FCC Communications Security, Reliability, and Interoperability Council, and DHS guidelines for security research touching on critical-infrastructure sectors. References to such frameworks are made for context only and do not imply endorsement, accreditation, certification or approval by any of the referenced bodies.
